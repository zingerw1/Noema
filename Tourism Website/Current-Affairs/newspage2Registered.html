Luxury Glamping Resort Opens in Makgadikgadi Pans

By Rachel Thompson • June 18, 2025 • 5 min read

A new luxury glamping resort has opened its doors in the stunning Makgadikgadi Pans, offering visitors a unique blend of comfort and wilderness experience. The eco-friendly resort features state-of-the-art sustainable technology while providing guests with an immersive salt pan experience.

Accommodation Features

The resort consists of ten luxury tented suites, each equipped with private viewing decks, outdoor showers, and climate control systems powered by solar energy. The tents are strategically positioned to offer panoramic views of the salt pans while ensuring privacy and comfort.

Sustainable Design

The resort operates entirely on renewable energy and employs a sophisticated water recycling system. Building materials were carefully selected to minimize environmental impact while maintaining the highest standards of luxury accommodation.

Guest Experience

Visitors can enjoy guided walks with San bushmen, quad biking adventures across the pans, and stargazing sessions with expert astronomers. The resort's restaurant serves farm-to-table cuisine featuring local ingredients and traditional recipes.

Resort Highlights:

  • 10 luxury tented suites
  • 100% solar-powered facility
  • Private viewing decks
  • Cultural experiences with San guides
  • Farm-to-table dining
  • Adventure activities available

By Sarah Mokgadi • June 16, 2025 • 4 min read

The annual Gaborone Food Festival returns this year with an expanded program celebrating the rich culinary heritage of Botswana. The week-long event will feature cooking demonstrations, tastings, and cultural performances that showcase the diversity of local cuisine.

Festival Highlights

This year's festival will feature over 50 local food vendors, traditional cooking demonstrations, and interactive workshops. Visitors can experience everything from traditional Setswana dishes to modern interpretations of classic recipes.

Event Details:

  • Daily performances and workshops

  • Craft demonstrations and sales

New Safari Guide Training Program Launches

By David Moyo • June 14, 2025 • 5 min read

A groundbreaking safari guide training program has been launched in Botswana, aimed at developing local talent and enhancing the quality of wildlife tourism experiences. The comprehensive program combines traditional knowledge with modern conservation practices.

Program Overview

The 12-month intensive training program covers wildlife biology, tracking skills, guest relations, and sustainable tourism practices. Participants will receive hands-on experience working alongside experienced guides in various national parks and private reserves.

Industry Partnership

The program is a collaboration between the Department of Wildlife and National Parks, leading safari operators, and international conservation organizations. This partnership ensures that graduates are well-prepared for employment in the tourism sector.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the program will be certified as professional safari guides and will have access to employment opportunities with leading tourism operators. The program also includes business management training for those interested in starting their own tourism enterprises.

Program Details:

  • 12-month intensive training
  • Professional certification
  • Hands-on field experience
  • Business management skills
  • Conservation education
  • Industry placement support

New Adventure Tourism Routes Launched in Northern Botswana

By Alex Thompson • May 15, 2025 • 5 min read

The Botswana Tourism Board has unveiled a network of new adventure tourism routes in the northern region, opening up previously unexplored areas to outdoor enthusiasts. The initiative includes guided hiking trails, mountain biking routes, and rock climbing locations, all designed to showcase the region's dramatic landscapes and diverse ecosystems.

Trail Network

The new network includes over 200 kilometers of marked trails varying in difficulty from beginner to expert level. Each route has been carefully mapped and includes rest points, viewing areas, and emergency access points. The trails offer unique perspectives of the region's geological formations and wildlife habitats.

Safety Measures

All routes are equipped with emergency communication points and are monitored by trained rangers. Visitors must register before starting any trail and are provided with detailed maps and safety briefings. Guide services are mandatory for certain high-difficulty routes.

Environmental Impact

The routes have been developed with minimal environmental impact, using existing animal trails where possible and implementing strict waste management protocols. Regular environmental assessments will monitor the impact of increased visitor activity.

Adventure Options:

  • Multi-day hiking expeditions
  • Technical mountain biking trails
  • Rock climbing routes
  • Guided nature walks
  • Photography tours
  • Camping facilities

Innovation

Botswana Launches Digital Nomad Visa Program

By Maria Santos • May 10, 2025 • 4 min read

Botswana has announced a groundbreaking digital nomad visa program, allowing remote workers to live and work in the country for up to two years. This initiative aims to diversify the tourism sector and attract long-term international visitors who can contribute to the local economy.

Visa Features

The new visa category offers multiple benefits, including simplified application processes, access to local banking services, and the ability to rent long-term accommodation. Digital nomads can also bring their immediate family members and are eligible for local healthcare coverage.

Infrastructure Development

To support the program, the government is investing in digital infrastructure, including high-speed internet connectivity in major cities and the development of co-working spaces. Several hotels and lodges are also creating dedicated facilities for remote workers.

Economic Impact

The program is expected to generate significant economic benefits through increased long-term stays, local spending, and knowledge transfer. Early projections suggest the initiative could attract up to 1,000 digital nomads in its first year.

Program Highlights:

  • Two-year visa duration
  • Simplified application process
  • Access to local banking
  • Healthcare coverage options
  • Family inclusion permitted
  • Co-working space access

Okavango Delta Lodges Pioneer Zero-Waste Tourism

By Thomas Green • May 5, 2025 • 5 min read

A consortium of luxury lodges in Botswana's Okavango Delta has launched an ambitious zero-waste initiative, setting new standards for sustainable tourism in Africa. The program aims to eliminate all non-biodegradable waste while enhancing the guest experience through innovative eco-friendly practices.

Waste Reduction Strategies

The lodges have implemented comprehensive waste management systems, including composting facilities, water recycling plants, and partnerships with local recycling initiatives. Single-use plastics have been completely eliminated, replaced with sustainable alternatives made from local materials.

Energy Innovation

Solar power systems now provide 100% of the lodges' energy needs, while innovative building designs maximize natural cooling and ventilation. Electric safari vehicles and boats are being introduced to reduce carbon emissions during wildlife viewing activities.

Community Integration

Local communities are central to the initiative, with residents trained in sustainable practices and employed in various roles. The program also supports local artisans who create eco-friendly amenities and decorative items for the lodges.

Initiative Highlights:

  • Zero single-use plastics
  • 100% solar powered operations
  • Water recycling systems
  • Electric safari vehicles
  • Local community employment
  • Sustainable supply chain
