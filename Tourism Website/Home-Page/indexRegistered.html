Botswana's Hidden Heritage Site

Discover the rich history and culture of the Tsodilo Hills located within Ngamiland District

Tsodilo Hills

Botswana's history dates back thousands of years, with evidence of early human habitation in the form of rock paintings and artifacts, especially in regions like Tsodilo Hills, often referred to as the "Louvre of the Desert."

In the late 19th century, the region was colonized by the British and was known as the British Protectorate of Bechuanaland. The British government took control to prevent the expansion of German and Boer territories.

Known for its large elephant population, Chobe National Park is a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

The Makgadikgadi Pans, remnants of a once vast lake, are another fascinating part of Botswana’s landscape.

Tsodilo Hills is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its ancient rock art, believed to be over 20,000 years old.
